War - How it started

Russia invaded Ukraine on February 24, 2022, in a major escalation of the Russo-Ukrainian War. Tens of thousands of people were killed on both sides, and Europe saw its worst refugee crisis since World War II. By late May, an estimated 8 million people had been displaced within the country, with 7.8 million Ukrainians departing the country as of November 8, 2022. Russian President Vladimir Putin declared a "special military operation" to "demilitarize" and "denazify" Ukraine. Putin advocated irredentist sentiments and questioned Ukraine's legitimacy as a state in his speech. Ukraine's president, Volodymyr Zelenskyy, declared martial law and ordered a mass mobilization.

War - How it is going

According to Indonesian President Joko Widodo, the G20 joint leaders' declaration was reached unanimously. "Most members vehemently denounced the war in Ukraine," adding that it is causing "immense human suffering." Individual attending leaders did not sign the 17-page paper, which acknowledged a difference of view. Following a missile strike on Poland on Tuesday, G20 leaders hurried to prevent a dangerous escalation in the crisis.

Sweden helping Ukraine

Sweden will provide three billion kronor ($286 million) in military aid and $69 million in humanitarian relief. The value of military help has not been announced. It will include an air defense system and ammunition. According to Prime Minister Ulf Kristersson, the assistance package is Sweden's largest to date. This is Sweden's seventh aid package to Ukraine since the conflict began in March 2014.

P.O.T.U.S.

On Tuesday night, former President Donald Trump revealed his intention to run for the Republican presidential nomination in 2024. Trump sought to inspire nostalgia for his presidency, juxtaposing his first-term triumphs with the policies of the Biden administration and the present economic condition. His statement comes as he attempts to recover the limelight following the GOP's lackluster showing in the midterm elections. He condemned the use of paper ballots, comparing America's electoral system to that of "third-world countries." But he also tried to broaden his grievances at times, lamenting Washington's "massive corruption" and "entrenched interests."

Word cup 2022

The 2022 FIFA World Cup will be held in Qatar from November 20 to December 18, 2022. This will be the first World Cup hosted in the Arab world and the second World Cup staged entirely in Asia, following the 2002 event in South Korea and Japan. This World Cup will be contested from late November to mid-December due to Qatar's extreme summer heat and humidity. Qatar will play Ecuador in the opening match at Al Bayt Stadium in Al Khor.
